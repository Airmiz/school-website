Course Description

AWR 201 builds on AWR 101 by inviting students to examine their relation to the community and the world. It teaches the conventions of academic research writing and guides students through an extended research project: project discovery, source annotation, drafting and revision, a polished final essay with proper citation, and a clear oral presentation. Because this is an accelerated 6-week course, students choose their own research topic.

AI Policy

AI use is prohibited in the Academic Writing classroom except where the instructor has given express permission. This covers all stages: summarizing, brainstorming, outlining, drafting, revision. Violations may result in a zero on the assignment, an Academic Integrity charge, or course failure.

Plagiarism Policy

All cases of plagiarism result in a zero on the assignment. Subsequent acts may go to the Academic Integrity committee and may result in failure of the course or suspension.
