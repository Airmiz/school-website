Process Work 1

CoR Ch. 1 + Wilkerson reading
Prompt

Course: AWR 201 · Instructor: Dr. Nadarajah

Read “I Don't Have to Love My Students to Be a Good Teacher” by Jherine Wilkerson and Chapter 1 from The Craft of Research, then respond to the prompts below.

  1. What central question is Wilkerson responding to, and how effectively does she answer it? Use one specific example.
  2. What “problem” does Wilkerson identify about the labor expectation of teachers, and why might it matter to educators?
  3. List two topics you'd consider researching in each category: College, Social Media/Pop Culture, Sports, Workforce/Labor.

Open the submitted PDF →

1. Wilkerson's central question

The central question Wilkerson is responding to is, is it the teacher's job to love the kids, is it okay to just do our job. Responding to her principle “'I want you to love your work and love the kids.' Why do I need to do anything other than what I am contractually obligated to do.” I would say that she does a very good job of explaining her position and gives very good examples. One thing I would say though is she leaves out in my opinion the biggest contributor to her discussion that is age. She is an elementary school teacher — the overall opinion is if you teach young kids, you must love the kids and your job. This stigma decreases as the students increase in age, and she does not talk about this. For example, where she says “My doctor doesn't love me, nor do my dentist or my therapist” — of course you're a grown person, but when you're a kid that stigma changes.

2. The labor-expectation problem

She addresses the problem that teachers must go above and beyond their job description to be considered good teachers — teachers are pressured into unpaid hours, emotional overextension, and a broken personal life. She says this is excused for teachers if you love the kids. She addresses a topic that has such stigma that it is not talked about. By writing this she is opening a discussion that allows other teachers to think about it and share their opinions.

3. Research topic ideas

College

  • Development of drug abuse
  • Peer pressure related to drugs

Social Media / Pop Culture

  • Glamorization of drug abuse
  • How social media starts or influences addiction

Sports

  • Drug use in sports
  • CTE

Workforce / Labor

  • Drugs in the workplace
  • AI
Common thread across these picks: drug use & addiction in different contexts — a strong jumping-off point for a research question.
